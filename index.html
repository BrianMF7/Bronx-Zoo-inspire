Bronx Zoo

The Bronx Zoo is in one of New York City most diverse borough which is the Bronx. In the Heart of the City, this zoo opened on November 8, 1899 which has the most unique and diverse animal and plantlife in the world. Over 10,000 + of animals, mammals, birds, fish, reptiles, and more that are 700 species. There are special events, amazing staff, great food and drinks and more specials that make this the best zoo to have animal lovers be here.

History

The New York Zoological Scoiety which was changed to the Wildlife Conservation Society was founded in 1985 with members of the Boone and Crockett Club. The main goal from this group to open zoos which aimed to preserved animalife and to emphasis on zoology. The Bronx Zoo opened on November 8, 1899 for the public and it had around 843 animals. Wiliam Temple Hornady became the first director of the Bronx Zoo which he had 30 years of experience here.

Facts

  • Its the largest Metropolitan Zoo and one of the most visited in the world.
  • Bronx Zoo has many names it can be reference too like Bronx Zoological Park or Bronx Zoological Gardens.
  • Fordham University used to own some land and sold it to the Bronx Zoo for $1,000.
  • Bronx Zoo had the first animal hosptial in a zoo which was opened in 1916.
  • We hosted Asmanian Tigers in the early 20th century.
